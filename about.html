Who We Are?

For me, what we think about who we are is inextricably connected with our interaction with others. There is something in behavioral psychology called “reinforcer”, and by definition; anything that follows a behavior that increases the chances of that behavior occurring again1. If I offer you a chocolate and you give me a compliment, “thanks” or “you are the best” I will most probably share it again. We communicate all the time, with friends, family, surrounding, etc. From that communication we receive and give; we give and receive some part of our personality.
